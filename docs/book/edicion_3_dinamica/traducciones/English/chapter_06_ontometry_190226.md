## 6.1 Introduction: Ontological Blindness

We live in a civilization that knows the price of everything and the value of nothing. This phrase, which used to be a poetic lament, is today a technical description of our economic crisis. Current systems operate with an **ontological blindness**: they are unable to distinguish, in their balance sheets, between life and inert matter, or between suffering and efficiency.

For the conventional market, an egg is an egg. Its value is determined by supply and demand. However, from the perspective of reality, an infertile egg (pure protein), an egg fertilized with 10 days of development (potential life), and an industrial egg produced under torture (vital debt), are radically different entities.

**Vital Ontometry** is born to cure this blindness. It is the set of methodologies and protocols that allow for the objective and verifiable measurement of the vital impact of any action, product, or service, within the theoretical framework of Maxocracy and under the principles of the Eight Axioms of Truth.

## 6.3 Definition of the Vital Footprint Vector (VFV)

The central tool of Ontometry is the **Vital Footprint Vector (VFV)**. Unlike a price (a single number), the VFV is a multidimensional matrix that breaks down the reality of cost.

**General Equation:**
$$VFV = (T, V, R)$$

Where **T, V, and R are multidimensional matrices** that decompose the collected information without any subjective weighting factor.

### Component T (Time)
Measures the consumption of conscious time (IVT).
$$T = T_{invested} + T_{inherited} + T_{maintenance}$$

- **T_invested**: Seconds of direct work objectively measurable.
- **T_inherited**: Accumulated time in the genealogy of the product/service.
- **T_maintenance**: Time required to preserve functionality.

**Methodological Principle**: Only conscious or directed time counts. Time of non-directed natural processes (like plant growth) counts as part of resources, not conscious time.

### Component V (Lives)
Measures the real biological cost with surgical precision.
$$V = \sum (\text{number of beings} \times \text{biological development factor})$$

**Established Classification**:
| Category | Description | Value |
|-----------|-------------|-------|
| **A (Resources)** | Infertile egg, processed plants | 0.0 lives |
| **B (Potential)** | Lives with partial development | Proportional to development |
| **C (Sentient)** | Organisms with full capacity of consciousness | 1.0 lives |

**Principle**: Biological development determines fractional value. A fertilized egg of 10 days has value = 10/21 â‰ˆ 0.47 of a complete chick. This eliminates both anthropocentrism and moral relativism.

### Component R (Resources and VDF)
Measures material consumption and ethical debt.
$$R = R_{direct} + R_{externalities} + R_{VDF\_infringed}$$

Here lies the most powerful innovation: **Ethical violations are accounted for as consumed resources.**

- **R_direct**: Energy, water, raw material used directly.
- **R_externalities**: CO2 emitted, pollution generated, depleted resources.
- **R_VDF_infringed**: Violations of the Vital Dignity Floor converted into quantifiable parameters.

If a hen lives in a cage that violates its *Vital Dignity Floor* (VDF), that deficit of space and well-being is accounted for as a "stolen resource" that must be paid for. Cruelty ceases to be "free" for the producer.

## 6.6 Physical Objectivity vs. Ethical Objectivity

A common critique of value theories is their subjectivity. Vital Ontometry resolves this by distinguishing two levels:

### Physical Objectivity (The Datum)
Measuring how much water was used, how many hours were invested, or how many animals died is a **physical fact**, verifiable and scientifically reproducible. The VFV captures this.

### Ethical Objectivity (The Threshold)
Defining what constitutes the *Vital Dignity Floor* (VDF) of a hen is an **ethical-scientific consensus** established through:
1. **Academic Consensus**: Validation by specialists in ethology and animal welfare.
2. **Community Deliberation**: Participation of relevant stakeholders.
3. **Empirical Validation**: Adjustments based on emerging scientific evidence.
4. **Transparency**: Documented and auditable process.

**Fundamental Principle**: Ethical parameters may evolve with knowledge, but physical measurement remains constant. Once the community defines the standard, violation of the standard is an objective datum.
