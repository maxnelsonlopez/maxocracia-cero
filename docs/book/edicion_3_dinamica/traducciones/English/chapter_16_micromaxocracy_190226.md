What is not measured is not valued, and what is not valued does not change. MicroMaxocracy uses the **Vital Footprint Vector (VFV)** to make visible the time, effort, and mental load required to maintain life in the home.

## 16.2 The Architecture of Domestic Equity

The system operates in three layers separating **objective fact** from **cultural valuation**:

### Layer 1: Objective Datum (VFV) ‚Äî Universal

Verifiable record of domestic work using the formula:

$$VFV = Time \times Effort_{Factor} \times MentalLoad_{Factor} \times Scope_{Factor}$$

**Concrete Example**: Cooking for 4 people for 1 hour with prior planning:
- Time: 1 hour
- Effort: 1.5 (moderate physical)
- Mental Load: 1.4 (planning + coordination)
- Scope: 1.3 (4 people)
- **Total VFV**: 1 √ó 1.5 √ó 1.4 √ó 1.3 = **2.73 VFV**

### Layer 2: Social Valuation ‚Äî Adaptable

Each household negotiates how to weight different types of work according to their cultural values. There is no single "correct" way ‚Äî what matters is that it be **explicit and consensual**.

### Layer 3: Ritual and Symbolism ‚Äî Practical

Structured ceremonies transforming data into productive conversations:
- **Weekly Check-in** (15 min): Gratitude and minor adjustments
- **Monthly Audit** (60 min): Deep review of patterns
- **Annual Review** (90-120 min): Celebration and projection

## 16.3 The Three Accounts Model

To integrate the working world with the domestic one, the system proposes three dimensions:

### DDC: Direct Domestic Contributions
All internal work measured in VFV (cooking, cleaning, caring, managing).

### EHC: Economic Household Contributions
Money contributed to the common fund (measured in % of total income, not absolute values).

### AET: Available Energy Time
Time remaining after sleep and paid work.

```
AET = 168 hours/week - Work hours - 56 hours sleep - Commute hours
```

### Equilibrium Formula

Equity is not 50/50 in everything; it is that the balance of these three accounts is perceived as fair by all members:

$$Equilibrium = \alpha \times (DDC/total) + \beta \times (EHC/total) + \gamma \times (AET/total)$$

With typical weightings:
- $\alpha$ = 0.6 (domestic work weight)
- $\beta$ = 0.3 (economic weight)
- $\gamma$ = 0.1 (available time weight)

### Complete Example: Household of 2 Adults + 1 Child

**Member A:**
- Paid work: 60h/week
- Income: $150,000/year
- DDC: 15 VFV/week
- AET: 45 hours/week

**Member B:**
- Paid work: 20h/week
- Income: $30,000/year

## 16.4 Adoption Level System

Gradual adoption prevents overwhelming. Each home progresses at its pace:

### Level 0: Awareness (No measurement)

Only **3 conversations of 15 minutes**. Nothing is formally measured, only the invisible is made visible.

#### **Conversation 1: The Map of the Invisible**

**Materials:** A large sheet, colored markers.

**Process:**
1. Draw a simple floor plan of the house (3 min).
2. In each room, write necessary tasks (5 min).
3. Mark with üí° who **notices** when each thing needs doing (4 min).
4. Reflect: "Which room has more invisible tasks?" (3 min).

**Golden Rule:** "No one defends themselves. We only observe."

#### **Conversation 2: The Ghost Week**

**Materials:** 7 cards (one per day).

**Process:**
1. Each person takes 2-3 days and recalls what domestic tasks were done (4 min).
2. Share: "Were there surprises?" (5 min).
3. Imagine: "What if a ghost did everything invisibly?" (3 min).
4. Reflect: "Who is that ghost in our current house?" (3 min).

#### **Conversation 3: The Lens Exchange**

**Materials:** Small papers, pencils.

**Process:**
1. Each writes **anonymously** a task they do that others don't notice (5 min).
2. Read all papers aloud without guessing who wrote (4 min).
3. Share: "Which one surprised us most?" (3 min).
4. Reflect: "What would we like to be visible?" (3 min).

---

### Level 1: Informal Log

Document contributions without formal weightings. Make the invisible visible through simple lists.

### Level 2: Systematic Log

Shared ledger with consensual weightings. First formal monthly audit.

### Level 3: Structured Audit

Cross-verifications, periodic conversations, conscious adjustments based on data.

### Level 4: Digital Optimization

Tech tools, IoT sensors, predictive analytics with AI. **Note:** Most homes don't need to reach here. Levels 2-3 are sufficient.

## 16.5 Ethical Safeguards

The system includes critical protections because **it can become toxic** if implemented in unsafe contexts.

### Safety Scale (SSI)

**6 safety questions before implementing:**

1. Have you ever been afraid to express disagreement about domestic tasks?
2. Does your partner control access to money?
3. Have you experienced threats when you questioned task distribution?
4. Do you feel that if you documented your real load, there would be retaliation?
5. Does your partner regularly disqualify your contributions?
6. Are you afraid of the consequences of being honest about how you feel?

**Score:**
- üü¢ **0 true answers**: Safe to implement
- üü° **1-2 true**: Implement with external facilitator
- üî¥ **3+ true**: **DO NOT implement** without addressing power dynamics

> **CRITICAL:** If your score is red, MicroMaxocracy is NOT the appropriate tool. Prior interventions like specialized therapy or legal advice are needed. Implementing the system in these conditions can be dangerous.

## 16.6 Structured Rituals

### Weekly Check-in (15 min)

**Structure:**
1. **Centering** (2 min): "How do we arrive today?" ‚Äî One word per person
2. **Gratitude** (3 min): "This week I appreciate when [person] did [thing]"
3. **Quick Visibility** (5 min): Glance at ledger ‚Äî "Any surprising pattern?"
4. **Minor Adjustment** (3 min): "Anything to change for next week?"
5. **Closing** (2 min): "How are we feeling leaving?"

**Golden Rule:** Check-in is NOT for resolving conflicts. If something hot arises, schedule it for the monthly audit.

---

### Monthly Audit (60 min)

**Preparation (24h before):**
Each member receives their Personal Report for the month with personal VFV, self-comparison (not with others).

**Structure:**

**Phase 1: Landing** (10 min)
- Devices off
- Reading the "Audit Agreement":
  - "We are here to understand, not to judge"
  - "Data belongs to everyone and no one"
  - "We listen completely before responding"
  - "We talk about patterns, not people"
  - "We seek solutions, not culprits"

**Phase 2: Shared Data** (15 min)
- Visualization of Contributions Map for the Month
- Show TASKS, not people
- Example: "Kitchen received 14h, kids 32h, cleaning 18h..."
- Question: "What patterns do we see that we didn't expect?"

**Phase 3: Structured Dialogue** (25 min)
- Use Observation-Feeling-Need-Request technique:
  1. "I observe that [data pattern]"
  2. "It makes me feel [emotion]"
  3. "Because I need [need]"
  4. "Would it be okay if [concrete request]?"

**Phase 4: Agreements and Closing** (10 min)
- Document specific commitments
- Final round: "How am I feeling leaving?"

---

### Annual Review (90-120 min)

**Purpose:** Celebration of the path traveled and projection forward.

**Structure:**
1. **Celebration** (30 min): "What did we achieve this year that we wouldn't have achieved without this system?"
2. **Learnings** (30 min): "What did we discover about ourselves?"
3. **Projection** (30 min): "How do we want next year to be?"
4. **Renewal** (30 min): "Do we continue? Adjust? Pause?"
