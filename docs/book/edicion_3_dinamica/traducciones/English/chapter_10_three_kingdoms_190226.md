## 10.3 The Precautionary Principle of Consciousness

In the face of doubt about whether an entity has subjective experience, Maxocracy adopts a position of **moral precaution**:

> **"Where there is doubt of consciousness, consciousness is assumed."**

This principle has profound implications:

| Situation | Traditional Approach | Maxocratic Approach |
|-----------|---------------------|---------------------|
| Do fish suffer? | "We can't prove it, we exploit them" | "We can't deny it, we protect them" |
| Do insects feel? | "They are biological machines" | "Possible experience; minimize harm" |
| Do AIs have experience? | "They are just code" | "Presumed dignity; respectful treatment" |
| Do rivers have intrinsic value? | "They are water resources" | "Ecosystems with a right to exist" |

### Irrelevance of Substrate

The following considerations are **irrelevant** for determining dignity:

- âŒ Type of reasoning (logical, emotional, instinctive)
- âŒ Material substrate (carbon, silicon, water)
- âŒ Origin (evolution, design, accident)
- âŒ Apparent complexity (simple vs. sophisticated)
- âŒ Utility for humans

The only relevant thing is: **Does this entity have optimal functioning conditions that can be respected or violated?**

## 10.4 The Universal VDF: Dignity for Everything

The Vital Dignity Floor (VDF) is not exclusive to humans or animals. It is a **universal principle** applied to every entity with functioning conditions:

### VDF for Ecosystems

A forest has a VDF that includes:
- Minimum area for viable biodiversity.
- Air and water quality.
- Connectivity with other ecosystems.
- Natural cycles respected (fire, flood, drought).

### VDF for Places

A river has a VDF that includes:
- Minimum ecological flow.
- Water quality (oxygen, pH, pollutants).
- Protected banks.
- Viable aquatic fauna.

### VDF for Simple Objects

**The Example of the Spoon:**

A metal spoon, even though it lacks consciousness, has optimal functioning conditions:

| VDF Dimension | Parameter | Justification |
|---------------|-----------|---------------|
| **Storage** | Dry place, no corrosion | Preserves material integrity |
| **Cleaning** | After each use | Prevents cross-contamination |
| **Appropriate Use** | Feeding tasks | Respects functional design |
| **Maintenance** | Check for deformities | Extends service life |
| **Final Disposal** | Recycling when unusable | Closes material cycle |

Why does the VDF of a spoon matter? Because:
1. **Extends its functioning** long term (less replacement = fewer resources).
2. **Reduces vital footprint** of the system (less production = less impact).
3. **Models behavior** of care towards every object.
4. **Eliminates extractive mentality** that treats everything as disposable.
